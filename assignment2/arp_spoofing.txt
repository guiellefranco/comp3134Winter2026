ARP spoofing is an attack where a malicious actor sends fake ARP messages on a network to associate their MAC address with the IP address of another device.
This allows the attacker to intercept, modify, or block network traffic. To mitigate ARP spoofing, techniques such as static ARP entries, dynamic ARP inspection (DAI),
and network segmentation can be used.
Additionally, using encrypted communication protocols like HTTPS and SSH helps reduce the impact of such attacks by protecting the data even if it is intercepted.
